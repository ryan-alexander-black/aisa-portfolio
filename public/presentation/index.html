01 · The big idea

This isn't about
doing more.

  • Yes, AI can help you do more — but for most of us, the best place to start is doing less.
  • It quietly takes the dull, repetitive, time-sucking work off your plate.
  • The admin, the typing, the chasing — it hands that time back to you.
  • And that time goes to what matters: your customers, growing the business, happier staff, and your life outside work.

Less of the boring stuff means more time for the rest of your life.

02 · What it actually is

A tireless
assistant.

  • Think of it as an assistant that's read more than anyone alive, never gets tired, and works for a few dollars a week.
  • It can't run your business and it has no real judgement — but it's brilliant at the boring 80%.
  • It's the thing that drafts the email, turns a voice memo into a quote, or answers the same question at 11pm.
  • And if you're not a computer person, that's exactly who it's for — you just talk to it in plain English, like sending a text.
03 · The honest bit

Is it coming
for jobs?

  • The honest answer is yes — some.
  • The roles most exposed are the ones that are mostly repetitive screen-work — admin, data entry, basic processing.
  • But that's only half the story…
03 · …but here's what matters

It can't run
without you.

  • It has no judgement, no accountability, and it doesn't really understand your business.
  • It's an assistant and a partner — it always needs a human holding the reins.
  • So the lesson isn't to be afraid; it's to be the one holding the reins.
  • If your role has a lot of repetitive work, become the person who uses AI brilliantly — that makes you more valuable, not less.

AI won't replace you — but a person using it might replace someone who won't. So be the one who uses it.

04 · What can it actually do?

Let's climb
a ladder.

  • It runs from "anyone could do this tonight" all the way up to "wait, it can do that?".
  • You don't need the top rung — but it's worth seeing how far it goes.
RUNG 1 — anyone, today

Write anything.

  • You never have to start from a blank page again.
  • The quote follow-up, the "closed Monday" notice, the reply you've been dreading — all drafted in about five seconds.
  • Then you just edit the draft, which is far faster than starting cold, and it still sounds like you.
  • It's free to try — ChatGPT, Claude or Gemini; just pick one.
RUNG 2 — a step up

Tidy anything.

  • It doesn't just write — it takes a mess and hands you back something clean.
  • Ramble a voice memo while you're driving and it becomes a tidy, professional message.
  • Snap a photo of your scribbled job notes and it becomes a typed checklist for the team.
  • Drop in a long, painful email thread and it comes back as a three-line summary.
RUNG 3 — "oh, it can do that?"

Find out anything.

  • It can go and do real research and real work for you.
  • In my own work, I point it at a website and know a business back to front in about ten minutes — that used to take hours.
  • For you, that's sizing up a competitor, researching a supplier, or prepping for a big meeting.
  • Or an assistant that answers your customers' questions 24/7, while the important ones still reach you.
THE WOW — where it can go

A brain for
your whole business.

  • One assistant connected to every part of your business — sales, customers, numbers, suppliers, processes.
  • You ask it anything — "which customers haven't ordered in 90 days?" — and get an answer in seconds.
  • A brain for your whole business — but it still can't run it. It's your partner, and you're in charge.
05 · The whole point

Let the machine do the busywork — so you're free to serve the people in front of you.

  • Every hour it hands back is an hour you're not buried in admin at 9pm.
  • It's an hour for the customer, for your team, for the people it's all really for.
